Devin Townsend

The Mad Scientist of Metal

1972: Born in British Columbia, Canada

1993: Records vocals for Steve Vai's Sex and Religion, embarks on a world tour of the album

1994: Receives a five-album contract offer from Century Media, recruits musicians to form industrial death metal band Strapping Young Lad

1997: Releases City with Strapping Young Lad and Ocean Machine: Biomech as a solo album, the former going on to be regarded as a groundbreaking industrial metal album

1998: Records and releases Infinity after being diagnosed with bipolar disorder

2002: Forms The Devin Townsend Band and begins splitting his time equally between this band and Strapping Young Lad

2007: Disbands both Strapping Young Lad and The Devin Townsend Band, gives up drinking and drugs. Later in the year, he releases a space rock opera entilted Ziltoid the Omniscient, highlighting his characteristic blend of humour and heavy music

2009: Announces a new venture called The Devin Townsend Project with four planned albums, and later in the year releases the first two: Ki and Addicted

2011: Releases the latter two albums of The Devin Townsend Project: Deconstruction and Ghost

2014: Records Casualaties of Cool, an ambient country-influenced album featuring Canadian musician Ché Aimee Dorval

For more information, check out Devin Townsend's Wikipedia page.
